Asian Small-Clawed Otter (Aonyx cinereus)

Profile

The Asian Small-Clawed Otter is the smallest otter species in the world, known for its playful behavior, intelligence, and unique feeding techniques. These otters are native to South and Southeast Asia, primarily inhabiting freshwater habitats, such as rivers, wetlands, and mangroves. They are distinguished by their small, nimble claws, which make them excellent at handling prey.

Physical Characteristics

  • Size: 47-61 cm (body length) with an additional 25-35 cm tail.
  • Weight: 3-5 kg.
  • Fur: Dark brown to grey with a lighter underside.
  • Unique Feature: Short claws that do not extend beyond their webbed fingers, aiding in handling food and digging.

Habitat and Distribution

  • Regions: Found in countries such as India, the Philippines, Indonesia, southern China, and Southeast Asian countries like Thailand and Vietnam.
  • Environment: Prefer coastal wetlands, mangrove swamps, rivers, and rice paddies.

Diet and Feeding Behavior

  • Primary Diet: Crustaceans, mollusks, small fish, and occasionally amphibians.
  • Feeding Technique: Uses front paws to find and manipulate prey, often using rocks as tools to crack shells.

Social Behavior

  • Social Structure: Highly social; lives in family groups of up to 12 individuals.
  • Communication: Uses vocalizations, scent markings, and body language to communicate.
  • Reproduction: Monogamous pairs; both parents care for offspring.
